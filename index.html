What This Is

Some builders work in public
before anyone is watching.

Amplification is ECCO's surface category for editorial amplifications of independent third-party builders. It is a registry, an incubation room, and a launch pad — a place to put attention and timestamped provenance on work that is already happening, before institutional recognition arrives.

The premise is simple. Most worthwhile work is done quietly, at the margins, by people who do not have a press team. The signal does not always come back. This surface exists to be one of the places it does — and to keep attribution intact when it does.

How It Works

Three tiers. Each subject, three ways in.

Soil
Tier I

The Archive

This surface · the soil

A registry entry. Short synopsis, attribution, posture flags, links to the deeper layers. The first place a subject becomes part of the public record on this surface.

Seed
Tier II

The Trailer

Narrative entry · the seed

A long-form editorial — typically an Echo Field Note — that walks the visitor into the subject's stake, situation, and significance. Optional. Where it exists, it lives at its own subdomain.

Fruit
Tier III

The Substance

The work itself · the fruit

The architecture, the research, the framework — in the subject's own design register. ECCO's editorial commentary layered around it; the underlying work itself remains the originator's. The deepest visit.

Amplification Posture · Surface-Level

Each subject on this surface carries its own posture. Some amplifications are unsolicited editorial commentary, with the originator's right of refusal explicitly preserved. Others may be invited collaborations, archive entries by mutual agreement, or memorials. The posture for any given subject is named on that subject's page. The surface itself is governed by a single principle: provenance stays with the originator. ECCO contributes the framing; the originator owns the work.

Provenance over performance. Infrastructure over influence. Doctrine over excuse.
